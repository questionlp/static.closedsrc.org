Simple Jalapeño Cheese Bread

Inspired by: Quick and Easy Cheese Bread
Preparation Time: 15-20 minutes
Cooking Time: 35-40 minutes
Serves: 12

Ingredients

  • 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 3/4 teaspoon kosher or sea salt 1
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black or white pepper
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne and/or mixed chile powder
  • 1 1/2 cups mild or sharp Cheddar, Monterey or Pepper Jack cheese, shredded
  • 4-5 medium jalapeño peppers, seeded and finely diced
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 3/4 cup 2% or whole milk
  • 1/3 cup grapeseed, olive, canola or vegetable oil 2

Instructions

  • Pre-heat oven to 400 degrees F. Lightly grease a non-stick 9x5-inch loaf or a four mini-loaf pan.
  • In a large bowl, combine the beaten eggs, milk, oil, salt, chile powder, peppers and grated cheese. Fold in the dry ingredients into the wet mixture until all ingredients have been combined. Over-mixing will lead to a dense and tough bread.
  • If using a 9x5-inch loaf pan, pour the batter into the prepared pan and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until golden and a toothpick inserted into the center of the loaf comes out clean. If using a mini-loaf pan, divide the batter into each mini-loaf and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until peaks are golden and a toothpick inserted into the center of a loaf comes out clean.
  • Cool the loaf while still in the pan for about 15-20 minutes. Remove from pan and leave to cool completely or serve while warm.

Notes

  1. If kosher or sea salt is not available, 1/2 teaspoon of table salt can be used.
  2. Do not substitute oil with melted butter, margarine or butter substitute.
